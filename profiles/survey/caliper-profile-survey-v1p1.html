Abstract

This document extends Caliper 1.1 by defining a Survey Profile that describes survey-related activities such as collecting student feedback data via online surveys about learning experiences and levels of engagement. Everyone learns differently with a mix of their own learning styles, and student feedback on their learning experience is a key source of data that can help glean insights into students’ learning styles and interests. Thus, student feedback data can complement students’ behavioral data for learning analytics – such as knowing about students’ perceptions of how effectively learning activities helped students’ learning and what specific areas in teaching and learning can be improved to enhance students’ learning experience, leveraging student interests and learning styles, to achieve learning objectives. A survey profile expands the pool of Caliper-instrumented learning data to enhance the learning analytics across the analytics continuum – descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive. The rest of this spec describes the specific components of the survey profile – survey-related events, actions and entities.

Overview

Design Goals and Rationale

The Caliper Survey Profile models a Person, typically a respondent or a rater, participating in a survey that solicites feedback about their learning experiences. Everyone learns differently with a mix of their own learning styles, and student feedback on their learning experience is a key source of data that can help glean insights into students’ learning styles and interests. Thus, student feedback data can complement students’ behavioral data for learning analytics – such as knowing about students’ perceptions of how effectively learning activities helped students’ learning and what specific areas in teaching and learning can be improved to enhance students’ learning experience, leveraging student interests and learning styles, to achieve learning objectives. The Caliper survey profile expands the pool of Caliper-instrumented learning data profiles to enhance the learning analytics across the analytics continuum – descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive, by allowing to incorporate student voices in a semantically interoperable manner in learning analytics.

Use cases

The Survey Profile supports the following use cases:

Analytics for monitoring and improving response rates: getting a sufficient number of feedback responses is a critical part of reliability of results for data-driven, informed decision making. A low response rate may decrease the statistical power of the data collected and undermine the reliability of the results.
Analytics for student engagement. Student feedback participation behavioral data is an input to the student engagement modeling (description, predictive, and prescription).
Analytics for predicting student retention and success. Student feedback on their learning experience is an input to predictive modeling of student retention and student success, combined with other data such as LMS behavioral data, demographics, learning performance (grades).
Analytics for instructional design and learning delivery. Student feedback data is an input to personalized learning and adaptive learning that incorporates student feedback, Example: learning analytics for diagnosing why students are not being able to complete one particular assignment or learning activities online within a certain amount of time and prescribing potential personalized interventions, measuring progress over time.
Analytics for identifying areas to improve teaching and learning effectiveness, validating the pertinent KPIs to measure progress, and prescribing potential corrective interventions.

Representing Caliper as JSON-LD

Individual Caliper events and entity describes are serialized as JSON-LD. IMS Global provides a remote Survey Profile JSON-LD context for mapping SurveyEvent terms to IRIs. Caliper message producers MUST reference this context in every Caliper 1.1 SurveyEvent and/or Query describe created and serialized for transmission to a target endpoint.

See the Caliper 1.1 specification [[!CALIPER-11]] for a discussion of JSON-LD and JSON-LD context handling.
